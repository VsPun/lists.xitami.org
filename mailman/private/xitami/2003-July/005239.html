If that is all you need to run (Blogs and forums), most of those things are
generally written in PHP and pre-packaged that way.  However, if you need
to do anything else (e.g. I have a site that is _supposed_ to support
hundreds of software packages, but can't because of PHP's limitations), PHP
is not sufficient as a scripting language for me.  When I say small, I mean
content-wise small, not usage/#-of-visitors small.  However, I should point
out that any site running multiple languages requires all of the people
behind-the-scenes to know all of those languages...which costs more for the
company as those people tend to get paid more than people who only know one
or two languages.  Nuclear Vision is easier to learn and use, IMO, than
PHP.  It is designed as a freeware ColdFusion alternative and not
necessarily a PHP replacement.  Anyway, the OP decided to use PHP...I was
just merely providing an alternative.
