> Is a static ip necessary? I use TZO

Nice but not necessary. Using TZO to associate your name address to your
hopping IP number will give momentary glitches, but works well for many
people. 

In particular: if someone hits right after your IP number changes, they won't
connect until TZO gets updated. Since you say "checks it once a minute", that
should never leave you invisible for long.

A bigger issue, and I am not sure how this works, is that various ISPs may
cache DNS records on their own DNS servers rather than keep going to a DNS
server in authority. That's standard practice, the authoritative DNS record
can give a suggested time of updating, and I suppose TZO must be setting their
update suggestion shorter than a DNS system full of hard-wired "permanent IP"
machines like mine. But other ISP DNS servers are free to ignore it, and use
cached DNS records as long as they want.
