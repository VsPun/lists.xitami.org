For one thing..if you're behind a router/firewall/NAT..you can't access =
your
network via public IP...it just won't work..don't try it.

As far as running a web server on residential accounts...cable companies
tend to not allow this because of a few reasons, which I'll try to =
explain
below to the best of my knowledge.

I know @home had very strict policies about servers period. They worked =
on
the idea that not everyone in each neighborhood who subscribed to cable =
was
going to use it (some places had neighborhoods, we had the entire =
county) at
the same time. So, they could wire a t1 to 200 people assuming that only =
20
or so would be on at once. The uplink was capped to prevent too much was
being used.

So if you had 3 or 4 users running servers getting a lot of traffic..it
slowed EVERYONE down.

With DSL it's a bit different..you pay for your bandwidth, and that's =
yours.
Granted the telephone company may have a t3 going to the main =
servers...they
get more if they need it. Some providers kick you off DSL.

I'm a Verizon subscriber. I have a standard 768/128 ADSL Residential
account. I currently run Xitami, M-Daemon Pro, and Serve-U Pro on my =
other
PC upstairs. Now..I have two IP addresses thanks to my hub and DHCP, so =
I am
able to visit my site via public IP and vhosts. I guess I'm just a lucky
one.
