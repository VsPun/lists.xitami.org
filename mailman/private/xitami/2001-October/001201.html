This is a topic that comes up over and over again, so I will explain
why it happens and what our company did to "fix" it.  The best part
is that I will keep the explanation and solution in layman terms.

Xitami's ftp server works fine, actually very well... as long as you
are not behind a firewall.  File Transfer Protocol is an older transfer
protocol that, normally, doesn't work well behind a firewall.

Here, we struggled with this back and forth, finally concluding that
there was no way we could operate without a firewall.  Our first
compromise was merely to disable the ftp portion of Xitami.  After
all, from our perspectives, the first time we tried Xitami was based
on the curiosity of a full-featured web server with a small footprint.
We had always seen the ftp server as a bonus.

Since that time, we realized that ftp definitely had it's place,
especially with remote administration.  Our solution was to install a
separate ftp server that had the capabilities to handle PASV
transfers from behind a firewall.  In our case, we didnt have much
time to investigate all the options, so we went on recommendation
and have great fondness for ServU FTP Server.  I don't want to write
anything that sounds biased, so I would appreciate if others who
have chosen alternative ftp options to express what ftp servers they
have that also work from behind a firewall.  And, if there are any
situations where people have managed to get Xitami's FTP server
to work, I am sure everyone at Imatix would appreciate hearing that
as well, at least for the sake of adding the information to the FAQ.

Personally, I appreciate the power of Xitami.  I know everyone
wants to have more integrated features added to Xitami.  But, from
my perspective, what most of the Xitami audience are running are
the freely available Xitami server.  Therefore, one cannot expect
Imatix to invest more time (which equates to money) into
developing more add-ons. When one looks at break-even rationale,
since we have a retail application for which we paid, an application
which advertises ftp, not stipulative ftp, I would have to say that I
would prefer that Imatix spend my money (therefore their time)
investing in better development of a currently advertised feature and
having it work to it's fullest potential.
