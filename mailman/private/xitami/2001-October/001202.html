Unfortunately, Alvaro, I think you are wrong on several counts here.  First, 
there is nothing better for promoting small businesses around the world 
than making a cost-efficient server that has SSL.  I would have to say that, 
as a business owner, SSL has greater benefits than just selling products 
and services on the internet.  For example, it serves as a venue by which 
our company can pass secure information back and forth between us and 
our clients.  As for remote administration, I am sure you are aware that the 
server does have its own remote web-based server administration.  On the 
other hand, if it had the capability to incorporate the Microsoft Server 
Extensions, I would have to agree with you.

Alvaro, I think you are confusing me as being representative of Imatix.  Like 
you, I am one of their customers.  Nonetheless, I would have to counter 
your presumption with one of my own:  people DO use the Xitami server for 
multiple reasons, one of the most prevalent is that it IS free.  There are 
other equally comprehensive, if not more comprehensive, web servers that 
are marketed in the lower cost ranges.  A good example off the top of my 
head would be O'Reilly's web server.  As a matter of course, O'Reilly's 
server, at last reference, has both SSL and incorporates MS Server 
Extensions.  If you are thoroughly displeased with Xitami, perhaps you 
should opt for this alternative.  On the other hand, for rapid installation, I 
have to admit that O'Reilly's server has a considerable learning curve on the 
installation, as opposed to Xitami.

written as if the first-time user is to know the intricacies of editing the config

see that I am one of those jaded Windows users.  We are the ones who are
accustomed to the pamperings of "wizards" and other helper applications/files.
 *NIX users are familiar with the processes of customizing usability through
the config files in everything they do.  Therefore, it is not accurate of you to
say that ALL Xitami newbies are at a loss.  I would bet the majority of stranded
newbies would be in the Microsoft user category.

Oy!  That is the beauty of Xitami!!!!!  If you have not realized that and you
