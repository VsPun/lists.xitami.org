In message <005701c0f41d$61f72670$a65c5f80@neelix>, "Matt Jaworski" writes:
>How do I link up the info in another drive?

Create an alias that points at that other drive (or preferably a
directory on it), eg:

[Alias]
   mydir=d:\mydir

Then you can use URLs like:

http://www.example.com/mydir/myfile.html

You can't use drive letters directly in URLs.  (And even indirectly you
can use them only in file:/// URLs which work ONLY on your local PC.)
