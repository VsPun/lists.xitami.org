while viewing the router's incoming log-table, i can clearly see inbound traffic reaching xitami on port-80 as expected.  while viewing the router's outgoing log-table however, it appears that xitami makes use of a very wide range of random outgoing ports, while transmitting it's response to corresponding ip-addresses.
 
since installing some proper router-log capture and viewing software, it's now very clear to see that i was confusing xitami's http-transmission port, with the outgoing service-ports which are logged by the router.  though i did not believe you at first jacob, the new software clearly indicates that xitami is indeed transmitting on port-80 like you said it was.
 
i now have a second question which does not pertain to xitami, but i feel that someone in this forum should be able to assist:
 
this router-logging software (obtained from the router manufacturer) is very basic, and offers very little flexibility for tweaking user-options and comfort settings.  it simply loads at start-up, maximized in a 'normal window', and begins capturing log-data from the router.  closing the window [x], minimizes this tiny utility to the system-tray... where it belongs.
 
i would like to edit the run-key to have this utility load hidden, and directly to the system-tray, but cannot seem to find the correct command-suffix for the task.  i would kindly appreciate any information which may assist me with editing this run-key correctly.  thank you.
