In addition, everything is coming back as HTTP/1.0 - the documentation says
Xitami Server supports 1.0, but then goes on to say Virtual Hosts support
HTTP/1.1 - why would the main server support one thing versus the virtual

You need HTTP/1.1 for virtual hosts, it's not available in the basic
protocol.
HTTP/1.1 also support the "keep-alive" directive (header).
That's basically the main difference between the two.

However, no system (client or server) cares for HTTP/1.1 or HTTP/1.0, they
just look at the other headers.
