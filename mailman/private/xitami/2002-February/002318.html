Personally, I have never tried to use subdomains in this setup yet,
although I will soon.  But, there are a few points to look at here
regardless.  So . . .

A) if you are using dns2go to port forward for you, notice that it only
forwards web requests with the www prefix.  So the URL
http://test.modernwonder.com <http://test.modernwonder.com/>  will not
port forward.  Dns2go will instead
try to send it to your ip on port 80 regardles of what you have it set
to in the client.

B) when someone tries to connect to you from near within the Road Runner
network, dns2go forwards them directly to your IP, so actually they end
up contacting you from within the Road Runner network and may not
encounter a port 80 block on their way.  We are probably far enough
apart that I still encounter a port 80 block on my way to you - so i do
not see your site.

C) when you use vhosting on xitami, you get a separate defaults.cfg file
for each one.  If this one is set to a portbase of 0, it is listening on
port 80 anyway.  If it has a higher portbase, you probably wouldn't be
able to connect to it because dns2go is not forwarding since there is no
www prefix.

Here's what I would try:
1) Change your vhost to www.test.modernwonder.com
<http://www.test.modernwonder.com/> 
2) Check the defaults.cfg in the folder that xitami creates.  Make sure
to set the portbase to 6000 or 8000 or whatever it is that works for
www.modernwonder.com <http://www.modernwonder.com/>  .
3) go ahead and register www.test.modernwonder.com
<http://www.test.modernwonder.com/>  at dns2go in the
multi-domain manager section of the site, and make sure it is "grouped"
with www.modernwonder.com <http://www.modernwonder.com/>  .  I'm not
sure if the www is required, but
you should at least register test.modernwonder.com.
4) wait about a day for things to sort themselves out . . .

Hopefully that helps, if not, let me know and I will play around some
here.
