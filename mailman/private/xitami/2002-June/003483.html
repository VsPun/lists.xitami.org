At 12:45 PM 6/21/2002 -0700, David Holm writeth:
>Actually if I remember correctly your setup would have to be like this:
>CertDomain.com
>CertDomain.com/Company1
>CertDomain.com/Company2
>CertDomain.com/Company3
>Etc.
>or:
>www.CertDomain.com
>www.CertDomain.com/Company1
>www.CertDomain.com/Company2
>www.CertDomain.com/Company3
>As to add even a www.CertDomain.com requires a new certificate I believe.

Well, that's true only if the cert isn't a *.CertDomain.com cert.  However,
according to the HTTPS RFC, only sub-domains work.  So, with a
*.CertDomain.com cert. the sub-domain my.www.CertDomain.com will fail a
browser check, but www.CertDomain.com won't.

That, of course, __assumes__ that all browsers follow the HTTPS RFC 100%.
