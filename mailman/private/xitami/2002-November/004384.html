> Xitami running on port 80. I also have a web-mail server on the
> same computer running on port 800. I'm trying to set things up in
> such a way

The only easy way to do that is to put a click-link on your port-80
main page: <A HREF="http://mydomain.com:800">Webmail!</a>

If your main page loads quickly, this is one-click for the users.

What -might- work slicker is a V-host main page that did a quick
redirect. The HTML below seems to work for me (with your real URL).
The user sees a black page with a link, but if the browser handles a
meta-redirect (and if your server is quick) they hardly see it at
all before your mail-page opens. If their browser doesn't take the
hint, they click on "Web-Mail" and get there.

<HTML>
 <HEAD>
  <TITLE>bouncing . . . .</TITLE>
<meta http-equiv="refresh" content="1; URL=http://mydomain.com:800">

 </HEAD>

<BODY bgcolor="#000000">
 &nbsp;
 <p>
 &nbsp;
 <p>
 <TABLE cellpadding="10" cellspacing="0" align="center" valign="center">
   <TR align="center">
     <TD width="100%" align="center" bgcolor=#E6E7E1>
Jump to <a href="http://http://mydomain.com:800">Web-Mail</a>
       </TD>
   </TR>
 </TABLE>

</BODY>
</HTML>

Otherwise it gets pretty messy.
