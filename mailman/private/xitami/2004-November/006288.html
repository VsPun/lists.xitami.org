Wouldn't a hardware router be better?  It doubles as a firewall and adds an
extra layer that doesn't have a visible OS under it.  I've been under the
general impression that software-based firewalls only somewhat work, but
I've not heard of anyone with a hardware router and a solidly closed
network (i.e. only port 80 open on the outside) having problems.  I tried
ZoneAlarm out myself once and wasn't too impressed (it worked, but it
wasn't easy to use), but that was two years ago.  I don't know why, but I
feel "safer" with a fairly decent, web configurable hardware router than
sitting directly on the Internet hoping everything on the software firewall
works fine.  I'm probably being confidently geeky because shiny hardware is
supposed to be impressive.
