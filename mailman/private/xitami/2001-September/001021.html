What Mr. Soulkoff is referring to:

In the defaults.cfg file, under the virtual hosts section:

<snippit>
[Virtual-hosts]
     www.mayanarch.com=mayanarch.cfg
     mayanarch.com=mayanarch.cfg
     www.thinkincircles.com=thinkincircles.cfg
     thinkincircles.com=thinkincircles.cfg
     www.simangroup.com=simangroup.cfg
     simangroup.com=simangroup.cfg
...
...
<snippit>

THEN, in your DNS server you must configure, A Records only:
     mayanarch.com    A    xxx.xxx.xxx.xxx
     www.mayanarch.com    A    xxx.xxx.xxx.xxx
    thinkincircles.com    A    xxx.xxx.xxx.xxx
    www.thinkincircles.com    A    xxx.xxx.xxx.xxx
    ...
    ...

This, of course, is assuming that xxx.xxx.xxx.xxx is the SAME IP address for
each of your VHosts.  Once the DNS reference is made for www.domain.com AND
domain.com then anyone who types either name into their browser will find
your server.  Once the request gets to your server, then the Xitami config
above will allow users, typing either name, to get to the correct site.

If you only have www.domain.com configured, and someone types
http://domain.com then that user won't ever get to your VHost because
www.domain.com and http://domain.com are physically TWO different DNS names
and they must each of their own DNS entry.  The same goes for Xitami.
