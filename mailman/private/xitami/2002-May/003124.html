In message <NBEMJPOLIJHPGPFFHOLLGEJGGHAA.nberg@gandalf.xs4all.nl>, "Nico Berg" writes:
>How to make Xitami make use of PHP4 under Linux Suse 7.2?[.....]
>[Filter]
>    Php = "\\usr\\bin\\php"
>[....]
>In Linux we normaly don't use double slash but single slash doesn't work
>either. 

Those are backslashes.  You want to use (forward) slashes.  It'll work
much better that way :-)

Eg, 

[Filter]
  php="/usr/bin/php"

Depending on exactly how your PHP was compiled/configured you might need
to fiddling with it's configuration or rebuild it to make it work in
Xitami filter mode.

You don't need the MIME setting; it's completely irrelevant.

>P.s. In the time I used Windows for this job the installation of PHP toke
>care of Xitami why not in Linux......?

Presumably since you used SUSE's installation of PHP, not PHP's one?
Otherwise, try asking the PHP folks.
