Nevertheless, including such a disclaimer on mails sent on the one-to-many
basis of a mailing list is consummately stupid, because YOU have no
control over the members of the set which your disclaimer calls "intended
recipients", since members may join and leave the list at any time, and
in most cases any legislation upon which you are relying is positively
NOT applicable to the "recipients".

I would advise anyone with such boilerplate inclusions to configure their
mailers so as NOT to send it when the addressee is a mailing list; ditto
for postings to newsgroups, where such disclaimers are equally risible.

PHBs require educating as to the differing modes in which e-mail might be
used: for them to issue an edict requiring such boilerplate on mails
which are not one-to-one (or one-to-few) merely reinforces just why they
ARE pointy-haired bosses.
