It sounds to me like your TZO client may be sending your router mapped IP, I
say this because if they are saying that they get that common network IP
address as the resolved address, it would have to be comming from TZO as
reported to them via client.  Perhaps a matter of the TZO client needing to
know what IP to be providing via the client.  (isp vs network).   I really
see no other way they would see your network IP externally.

You can test this theory by going to the control panel at TZO and placing
your current ISP address manually into the given entry field they provide
for doing this manually- (turn off the tzo client so it doesn't override it
again).

See if it then works- If you want to contact me via email directly it may
prove faster- I've run xitami, and about everything under the sun with my
TZO setup so I'm sure we could find the issue with a few wacks at it.

This doesn't take into account any mapping you may need to do for ports, but
I'm not sure that's even your issue here.

Jack
