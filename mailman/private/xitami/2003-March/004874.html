Moreover, the punched card code had no means of generating what is
correctly called a backslash.  The character is simply a "slash".

> "\" - this is the forward slash.  Again, describing a physical motion.

That character was never seen until the second (or was it the third?)
revision of ANSI X3.64 (aka ASCII) in the mid-to-late-1960s.  As soon as
it appeared, some people started calling it a "backslash", because it was
the mirror image of the familiar "slash" character aforementioned.  Other
names are "reverse slash" or "reverse solidus".
