Scott,
    As has been mentioned here, and I agree completely, each person will
have their favorite tools.....that said, I'd reply to your comment by saying
"you always get what you pay for"  :)

   PB comes with tools that parse form data very easily.  You basically use
a string buffer to put the contents in, then strip off the variable values.
To create the resulting page, you use a line similar to:

WriteCGI "<b>The result of your query " & Request$ & " is: " & ResultValue$
& </b>"

The real power happens between the post/get data and the WriteCGI sections.
Execution speed, even opening a sequential file, reading through it for a
specific value, closing and processing it is amazing, even on low end
hardware.  I'd be more than happy to email you some samples I've wipped
together.  There is also a ton of very impressive source code on the PB user
forums on PowerBasic's web site.

It all comes down to what you're comfortable with.  I think Bob Zale has
created an awesome product (ever since Borland TurboBasic), the support is
exceptional, and even though its not free, I find I can do things with PB
faster and easier than any other tool *most of the time* and to me, that's
worth paying for.   Your mileage may vary.  :)

--Joe
