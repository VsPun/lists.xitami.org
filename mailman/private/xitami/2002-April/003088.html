I believe (BICBW) that "sb" is an abbreviation for "somebody".  I see
this abbreviation used in many newsgroups, by German speakers writing in
English.  I cannot remember ever seeing it used by anyone for whom
English is their first language (no, not even by a schoolkid 'sndng
txt msgs 2 hs m8t usng SMS' :-)

One also sees "so" for "someone" as well.  Quite where this usage
originated, I don't know; certainly neither abbreviation is to be found
in any dictionary of the English language.   (To me, "sb" is an
abbreviation USED BY dictionaries, for "substantive", whilst "so" is
sometimes seen in newsgroups, as "SO", for "significant other".)
