In message <3CA5D6B7.30198.163F21E@localhost>, "WhiteShepherd" writes:
>One: some of those connecting will be broadband connections.  Won't the 
>broadband connections take the lion share of the bandwidth (being able to 
>take more faster in shorter time frame) leaving the dialups with the scraps 
>of what they can down feed in a bandwidth crunch?  

You might want to experiment with the bandwidth shaping code in the Xitami
2.5 beta.  Read up on the pipe definitions files for how to do this.
That way you can limit connections to, say, a modem-and-a-bit worth
each, or something like that.

>My second question is I saw in the faq that EMX was limited to 40 open 
>sockets in the faq.  This has been in the faq for a LONG time.  Has the 
>newer versions of EMX made improvements on this?  

This is 40 sockets per process.  And it's just a default setting.  You
can tell EMX to allow space for more sockets for the process at runtime
by setting an environment variable; look at the EMX documentation for
details.
