> open control panel and open the xitami cpl. Is there a more
> comfortable way to do this, similar to the system tray icon?

Can't you put a Shortcut to xitami.cpl on your desktop or in your
Launch bar? A "CPL" file is really just an EXE file.

I'm not at an Xitami machine but I can do this:

Start, Settings, Control Panel.

Right-click on Mouse (or Xitami?), an option is "Create Shortcut".
Do that, it tell you that the shortcut is created on the Desktop (I
guess it does not want ControlPanel directory cluttered).

Now find your Desktop, drag the Mouse shortcut to the LaunchBar
(normally populated with IE and Outlook). I click that and I can
twiddle my mouse's properties.

Same should work for the Xitami CPL ?
