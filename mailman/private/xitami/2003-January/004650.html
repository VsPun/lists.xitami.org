At 19:36 26/01/03, you wrote:
>Translation. Could be a hardware problem.
Not necessarily. Could be a silent crash of part of the core operating 
system, a power glitch or a power outage... ...
Log files that are kept continuously open are more prone to this 
phenomenon, since there is never a CLOSE forcing a buffer flush (and 
synchronisation between actual data and FAT information).
It will also more likely occur on a FAT drive than on a NTFS drive (with 
it's backtracking ability)
