The dll is named the same as its larger exe brother..call the dll in the script and the exe is run, which in turn returns the page. I realize now that this applies only for IIS. I had used o'Reilly in the distant past [ and may have to use it again ??] and it recognized the the .exe extension. Xitami appears to make a leap of faith that this is a script [ given the cg-bin in the tag ]

 What I've tried is to NOT use the dll extension and call the exe directly..but use just the file name instead. cgi-bin/MZload1 with no file extension. I get a blank page returned. Look on the bright side at least it found something this time out, and judging by the brief delay it looks as if something is being run.

> CGI runs executable files -- not DLLs. DLLs are not directly
> executable. As the name infers, they are Dynamic Link
> Libraries that are used by applications.
> 
> Your DLL is likely an
> ISAPI DLL designed to work with IIS. This has some performance
> benefits since the DLL is loaded once by the IIS exe into
> its memory space, whereas CGI exes must run-execute-close
> each time they are needed.
> 
> 
> Further, a CGI exe must
> conform to the input/output requirements of CGI.
> Try calling your exe directly.
