No it doesn't; OS/2 expands wildcards within each application, not within
the shell.  So the parameter "*.c" would be passed over intact to the
c.cmd (is this a batch file, or is it a proper REXX command procedure?)
and this in turn (presumably) will pass it on to gcc --- which doesn't
know how to expand "*.c".

[If c.cmd *is* written in REXX, rather than just a series of OS/2
commands, then it would be easy to rewrite so as to expand *.c]
