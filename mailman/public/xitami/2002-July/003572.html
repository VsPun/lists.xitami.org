Depending on what you are using for a net connection, your internet provider gives a 
Dynamic or Static IP.  It is not a setting you really chose unless your internet provider 
has definatly provisioned you a static ip.  If you "do" have a static IP you'll need your IP, 
your netmask, their router #, and their DNS #s.  I forget the name of the config utility for 
setting this up on the ethernet under linux (been a bit and I haven't used linux that 
much).  But I'm sure someone could tell you.  Just make shure you have your required 
information from your provider.
