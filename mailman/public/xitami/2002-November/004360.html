ARE you registered in any DNS? Do you have a domain registration for
something like www.joc.com? If not, then there isn't any way to
avoid using the IP number as your "name". You need a unique domain
name, and its IP needs to be knowable to anyone who wants to see
your site.

Pay the $35 to have a domain name registered. Then pay your ISP to
enter your domain name in their DNS and pointing to your IP number.

There are ways to avoid dealing with your ISP, especially if they
ban servers on your class of account, or if they deliberately change
your IP number from time to time (to make it hard to run a server on
a low-price account). I've never dealt with that, but others here
know how free-market Domain and dynamic-DNS works.
