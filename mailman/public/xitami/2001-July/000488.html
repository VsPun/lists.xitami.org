I've had that problem on a couple of systems and it's very annoying!
However, I got it to work by:

1) Installing PHP to C:\PHP (it shouldn't matter where it is installed)
2) Copying PHP.EXE and PHP4TS.DLL to the Xitami directory
3) Adding:

.php=php.exe
.php3=php.exe

to the [Filter] section of the appropriate CFG file.
