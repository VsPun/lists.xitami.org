Go to Administrative Tools > Services and find Xitami in the list. Then
double-click it to bring up its properties and look for recovery options in the
Recovery tab. You will see that, in case of a crash, you can choose to
automatically restart the Xitami process or even reboot the computer. As a quick
reminder, this is only for Windows 2000 and XP (and maybe NT? I've never used
NT...).
